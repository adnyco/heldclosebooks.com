The Memoir

A witness story shaped in two-hour increments.

When Judy Wright received the call, she was not asked to save a child. She was asked to hold her until she died.

Instead, a long vigil began.

“Michigan Foster Parents of the Year — for sustained commitment to medically fragile and high-need children in Oakland County, Michigan.” Michigan Supreme Court · State Award, 2012

Just Hold Her is drawn from Judy's contemporaneous spiral notebooks during nearly two years of caring for a medically fragile foster child whose life was never expected to last. In a house governed by feedings, glucose readings, insulin, appointments, and alarms, daily care became both discipline and devotion.

This is a memoir of foster care, maternal presence, and the friction between lived experience and institutional certainty. It moves through medicine, bureaucracy, attachment, and the quiet endurance required to stay when no one can promise the outcome.

It is not a rescue story. It is a witness story.

Readers of When Breath Becomes Air, The Bright Hour, and The Glass Castle will find a new companion in this debut memoir.

About the Author

Judy Wright

Mother · Foster Mother · Advocate

Judy Wright is a mother and foster mother whose life has been shaped by the demanding, ordinary work of staying.

Over twelve years in Oakland County, Michigan, Judy and her late husband John fostered fifty-five children, many of them medically fragile. Their home became known for taking the calls that required steadiness, patience, and unusual care.

Just Hold Her is her debut memoir, drawn from the notebooks she kept while caring for a foster child whose life was never expected to last.

Note on the Scholarship

The John and Judy Wright Scholarship for Foster Children

Established in partnership with the Ennis Center for Children, the scholarship supports foster youth pursuing higher education in Michigan. It bears John Wright's name in recognition of the shared conviction that shaped their household for more than three decades.

John Wright passed away before the manuscript was complete. The memoir is, among other things, a record of what he helped build.

The Imprint

The Architecture
of Presence.
Stories found in the margins.

Held Close Books is an independent literary imprint dedicated to stories of caregiving, witness, and the quiet endurance of love. We publish a small number of deeply personal works — narratives born from lived experience, written from the inside of life's most difficult and most ordinary moments.

Our purpose is singular: to reclaim the narrative of caregiving from institutional protocol and return it to the people who actually do the work. We publish the stories that live in the margins of clinical charts and court records, in the spiral notebooks kept at two in the morning.

We are a curated imprint with an intentionally small list. We are not accepting unsolicited manuscripts at this time. Inquiries may be directed to the media contact below.
