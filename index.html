The Book

A foster mother's nightwatch.
Built from the notebooks.

That was the call. Two months old. Four pounds. Blind, deaf, and tethered to monitors, Jayden arrived with thirty diagnoses and no plan. They didn't ask us to save her. They asked us to stay.

“Michigan Foster Parents of the Year — for sustained commitment to medically fragile and high-need children in Oakland County, Michigan.” — Michigan Supreme Court – State Award, 2012

Just Hold Her is the relentless, true story of a foster mother's 674-day nightwatch. Built from the spiral notebooks kept during a two-year vigil, Judy Wright's literary debut captures the collision of medicine, bureaucracy, and human attachment.

In a house governed by two-hour feeding cycles and the shrill cry of medical alarms, Judy traded "big hope" for small mercies. While the system prepared for the end, morning kept arriving — and with it, the quiet defiance of ordinary labor: warming the bottle, checking the numbers, and asking the one question no one else was still asking.

This is not a rescue story. It is a witness story. A memoir for the caregivers, the foster parents, and the medical staff who live at the edge of what medicine can do. For anyone who has carried responsibility without certainty, or learned that faith arrives not as a guarantee, but as presence.

Readers of When Breath Becomes Air, The Bright Hour, and Being Mortal will find a new companion in this debut memoir.

The Imprint

The Architecture
of Presence.
Stories found in the margins.

Held Close Books is an independent literary imprint dedicated to stories of caregiving, witness, and the quiet endurance of love. We publish a small number of deeply personal works — narratives born from lived experience, written from the inside of life's most difficult and most ordinary moments.

Our purpose is singular: to reclaim the narrative of caregiving from institutional protocol and return it to the people who actually do the work. We publish the stories that live in the margins of clinical charts and court records, in the spiral notebooks kept at two in the morning.

We are a curated imprint with an intentionally small list. We are not accepting unsolicited manuscripts at this time. Inquiries may be directed to the media contact below.

Author

Judy Wright

Author · Foster Mother · Advocate

Judy Wright is a foster mother, caregiver, and advocate whose life has been shaped by the demanding, ordinary work of staying. She met her husband, John, through aviation. He was a flight instructor, a Vietnam veteran, and later a corporate pilot whose career took him around the world. What bound them most deeply was a shared conviction that children mattered — not abstractly, but concretely, in the daily decisions of a household.

Over thirty-seven years of marriage, that conviction became the center of their life. They raised four children while navigating years of relocations tied to John's flying career. When their youngest was sixteen, Judy and John began fostering. What started as short-term placements grew into twelve years of long-term work inside Michigan's foster care system — caring for infants and toddlers whose lives had already been marked by instability, medical trauma, and loss. Over those years, they fostered more than fifty children, many of them medically fragile.

Their home became a place of readiness. Bins were labeled by size and season. Bassinets were carried up from the basement in the middle of the night. Judy learned to advocate inside systems that often failed to see children clearly, asking the same questions again and again, showing up when others did not, and loving without guarantees.

After John's death, Judy turned to writing — not to explain foster care or defend it, but to bear witness to what it costs and what it gives. Just Hold Her is drawn from the spiral notebooks she kept during a two-year vigil. It is not a story about saving. It is a story about staying.

Impact

Extending the Commitment.

The work of Just Hold Her does not end with the book. Held Close Books and the Wright family are committed to ensuring that the story's legacy extends into tangible support for the children and communities it depicts.

In collaboration with the Ennis Center for Children, the John and Judy Wright Scholarship was established to support foster youth pursuing higher education. It is a direct extension of the conviction that has always animated the Wright household: that children matter, concretely, in the decisions that shape their futures.

A portion of proceeds from Just Hold Her supports the scholarship fund. Readers who wish to contribute directly may do so through the Ennis Center.

Note on the Scholarship

The John and Judy Wright Scholarship for Foster Children

Established in partnership with the Ennis Center for Children, the scholarship supports foster youth pursuing higher education in Michigan. It bears John Wright's name in recognition of the shared conviction that shaped their household for more than three decades.

John Wright passed away before the manuscript was complete. The memoir is, among other things, a record of what he helped build.
